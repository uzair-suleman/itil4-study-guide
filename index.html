🔑 Foundational Concepts

Essential Terms You MUST Know

Service Management: A set of organizational capabilities for enabling value to customers in the form of services.
Value: Perceived benefits, usefulness, and importance of something to customers.
Service: Value co-creation by creating outcomes for customers they want to achieve without managing specific costs and risks.
Customer: Person who defines requirements for service.
Utility: Fit for purpose - service does what it's meant to do.
Warranty: Fit for use - service does this good enough (availability, capacity, continuity, security).

🧮 THE VALUE FORMULA

Value = Utility + Warranty (+ Perception)

📋 The 7 Guiding Principles

These guide organizations in ANY circumstances - even for implementing ITIL 4!

🎯 Focus on Value

Everything in the organization should be useful

🏁 Start Where You Are

Try to reuse existing resources instead of reinventing the wheel

🔄 Progress Iteratively with Feedback

Don't do everything at once, take baby steps

👁️ Collaborate & Promote Visibility

Involve the right people at the right time and gather factual data

🌐 Think and Work Holistically

Nothing ever alone, think about the effect of your actions

✨ Keep it Simple and Practical

Don't overcomplicate

🤖 Optimize and Automate

Maximize the value of human work

🔗 Service Value Chain

Turns demand into value through products & services

🔄 Six Activities (In Order)

  1. Plan - Make sure everyone understands direction, policy & plans
  2. Improve - Continuously improve all services, practices & activities
  3. Engage - With customers/users/partners to understand needs
  4. Design & Transition - Design new or changed services and move to production
  5. Obtain/Build - Get or build the needed service components
  6. Deliver & Support - Operate & support live services for users

🛠️ Key Management Practices

🔄 Change Management

Purpose: Maximize # of successful changes through proper risk assessment and minimize negative impact of failed changes

Types:

  • Standard: Pre-authorized, low risk, low cost
  • Normal: Authorization depends on what calendar changes
  • Emergency: Needs rapid action, key business separate changes

🚨 Incident Management

Purpose: Minimize negative impact of incidents by restoring normal operation ASAP

Incident: Unplanned interruption or reduction of quality

Swarming: May help with complex issues

🔍 Problem Management

Purpose: Reduce likelihood of recurring incidents by identifying root causes

  • Problem: Unknown cause of one or more incidents
  • Known Error: Problem with known root cause but no solution yet
  • Workaround: Alternate solution, reducing impact

🎧 Service Desk

Purpose: Capture demand for incidents & service requests. Single point of contact

Skills: Incident analysis, prioritization, effective communication

📊 Service Level Management

Purpose: Set clear business-based targets for service performance

  • SLA: Agreement between customer & Service Provider
  • OLA: Agreement between different units of same organization
  • UC: Agreement between Service Provider and external Supplier
